Derek Fisher (born August 9, 1974) is a former professional basketball player and coach in the United States. Fisher was a professional basketball player for 18 years, spending the majority of his career with the Los Angeles Lakers, with whom he won five NBA titles.

He also spent time with the Golden State Warriors, the Utah Jazz, the Oklahoma City Thunder, and the Dallas Mavericks. He has also served as the National Basketball Players Association’s (NBPA) president.

Who is Derek Fisher?

Derek Fisher was born in the city of Little Rock, Arkansas. He was a basketball letterman at Parkview Arts and Science Magnet High School in Little Rock.

He went on to study communications at the University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UALR) for four years. Fisher finished his college career at Arkansas-Little Rock second all-time in points (1,393), assists (472), and steals (189).

Over 112 games, he averaged 12.4 points, 4.4 rebounds, and 4.2 assists, and he topped the club in assists and steals every year. Fisher also set a school record for career free throws made (399) and finished third all-time at UALR in three-point field goals made (125).

He was named Sunbelt Conference Player of the Year as a senior, averaging 14.5 points, 5.2 rebounds, and 5.2 assists per game.

Fisher donated $700,000 to UALR in 2005 for the development of the Jack Stephens Centre auxiliary gym, which is now named in his honor, as well as the Fisher Fellows Life Skills program, a mentoring series for UALR student-athletes.
