Babies are known for their adorable coos and giggles, but they also make a variety of other sounds that can be confusing to new parents. In this article, we’ll explore some of the most common baby noises and what they mean.

Crying

Crying is the most common sound that babies make, and it can mean a variety of things. Sometimes babies cry because they’re hungry or tired, while other times they cry because they’re uncomfortable or in pain. If your baby is crying excessively, it’s important to talk to your pediatrician to rule out any underlying medical issues.

Gurgling

Gurgling is a sound that babies make when they’re happy and content. It’s often accompanied by smiles and coos, and it’s a sign that your baby is feeling comfortable and secure.

Sneezing

Babies sneeze frequently, and it’s usually nothing to worry about. Sneezing is a natural reflex that helps clear the nasal passages of mucus and other irritants.

Hiccuping

Hiccups are another common sound that babies make, and they can be quite loud and startling. Hiccups are caused by spasms in the diaphragm, and they usually go away on their own after a few minutes.
