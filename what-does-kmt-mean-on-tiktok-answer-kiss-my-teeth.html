What Does KMT Mean On TikTok? Answer Kiss My Teeth

Publish date: 2024-07-09

The things that go so viral on the internet are as unpredictable as their userbase. What does KMT mean on TikTok? Learn more below to find out.

The limitation to going viral on Tiktok seems to be just human imagination. People, songs, and even phrases like KMT see a spike in popularity now and then. It is very tough to keep up with the changing times.

If you do not know what KMT means, we’ve got you covered.

What Does KMT Mean On TikTok?

KMT is just a simple abbreviation of Kiss my teeth.

It is a figure of expression used to express disdain, disgust, anger, or annoyance. However, the phrase shot up in popularity in TikTok because of a song.

The song ‘Missing You’ by MBoogz9 uses the phrase in his lyrics. The song goes, “She sends me a text like ‘Boogz, are you missing me? But of course, I do; I message her back like K-M-T.”

Many Tiktokers are lip-syncing just to this line of the song, and it has tremendously shot up in popularity.

The lyrics are very relatable, and the catchy tune adds even more flavor. No wonder it has shot up in popularity.

KMT Urban Dictionary Meaning Explained

KMT was a slang added to the Urban Dictionary around 2009-2010.

The meaning hasn’t changed much over the years, but the slang has gotten more popular.

People have been abbreviating phrases since the early 2000s, and the slang and abbreviations add relish to texting.

If you don’t know why Kiss my teeth means to express disdain or annoyance, watch this Tiktok, you will know precisely why it is.

KMT is a Jamaican way of expressing disdain, and the action has been translated to a figure of speech.

KMT Meaning On Texting

KMT has the same meaning overall in texting as well, but the disdain or anger is usually feigned.

It is an excellent addition to letting people know that you’re annoyed in a fun way, and most often than not, people use it in a joking manner.

Like all other abbreviations used in texting, it is usually used in a manner of lighthearted talking. If you are truly angry, you wouldn’t need a figure of speech to show it.

Sometimes, not knowing the meaning of the slang means not keeping up with the conversation.
