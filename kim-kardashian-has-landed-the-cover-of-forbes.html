Kim Kardashian West Forbes Magazine Cover

Publish date: 2024-07-14

Kim Kardashian West wears many hats: reality TV star, naked selfie enthusiast, style icon, mom to Saint & North, wife of Pablo and more. Now, Forbes has dubbed her a "Mobile Mogul" due to the astounding success of her mobile game and various platforms. Kim Kardashian Hollywood, the reality star's mobile app game, raked in $45 million alone since its 2014 launch.

Kim revealed her Forbes cover on Instagram today, expressing how proud her late father Robert Kardashian would be. She capped the post with the hashtag #NotBadForAGirlWithNoTalent, a nice little dig at all those who like to remind West that she isn't famous for a conventional talent.

In the interview, Kardashian discusses the success of her virtual empire and how she manages to keep up. See her thoughts on the industry, what she has to say to haters and what's next—then read the full article here.

On integrating her real life into the Kim Kardashian: Hollywood game: "I should take a photo of myself in the Pablo one-piece, and you can use that."

When she realized tech was the next move: "I became really intrigued with the tech world. I started spending a lot of time in San Francisco. I realized this is really going to be the next cycle of my career and this is what I want to focus on."

On her personal tech habits: "I love my big computer, but I can't even tell you the last time I sat in front of it. [...] I'm always on my phone."

On her relationship with video games: "I loved video games, growing up. I remember I asked Kanye, 'Should I do this?' He was like, 'Yes!' That's how he got into music, because he wanted to do music for video games and wanted to create video games."

On her initial hesitations about the game: "I was like, 'I wonder if people are really going to…mimic my life.' "

How the game mimics her real life style: "The whole idea was to make it feel as live-time as possible," she explains. "I would give [Glu] bikinis and be like, 'Hurry up and mock up this bikini, because I'm going to wear it, and then you can have it live in the game!'"

On people bashing her entering the tech world: "When people looked at me in a way like, 'Why is she stepping into the tech world? That's not her territory! Stick to reality TV!' I was like, 'No.' " This is fun for me. Now I'm coming up with Kimojis and the app and all these other ideas. I don't see myself stopping."
